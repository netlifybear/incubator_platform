# Positioning

This document describes the strategic framing of the platform: where it sits in the market, how it creates defensibility, and what it could become. Use it for pitch decks, investor conversations, and competitive positioning. Do not confuse it with the product narrative in `story.md` — that document is for founder-facing and internal alignment; this one is about ambition and business model.

---

## The Analogy

The platform is to founder credibility what a credit bureau is to financial creditworthiness. Both solve the same core problem: **information asymmetry** between someone who has experience and someone who needs to trust that experience.

| | Credit bureau | Incubator platform |
|---|---|---|
| **Inputs** | Financial data from lenders, banks, card companies | Contribution data from founders — reviews, recommendations, tags, backlinks |
| **Process** | Verifies and standardizes into a credit report | Verifies identity and standardizes into a credibility report |
| **Output** | A score and report that lenders trust | A credibility report and signed packet that VCs and partners can verify |
| **Downstream** | Lenders, landlords, employers make faster trust decisions | Investors, partners, future founders make faster trust decisions |

In both cases, the intermediary reduces the cost of trust. Without a credit bureau, every lender runs their own background check. Without this platform, every VC does their own reference calls.

---

## Strategic Strengths

The analogy maps well onto the platform's structural economics:

- **Network effects** — each additional incubator that feeds data into the system makes the output more valuable to downstream consumers (VCs, partners), which gives more incentive for incubators to join.
- **Data moat** — the verified contribution history is both proprietary and compounding. Every cohort adds more data; older data becomes more valuable as the track record deepens.
- **Standardization value** — without a common format, every downstream party has to interpret raw data. A standardized credibility report with verified identity is the same unlock that standardized credit scores provided.
- **Familiar mental model** — investors already understand the credit bureau business. They immediately grasp the trust intermediary role, the data flywheel, and the defensibility.

---

## Limitations & Risks

The analogy breaks down in critical ways that must be acknowledged:

| Risk | Description |
|---|---|
| **Coverage** | A credit bureau covers a market. A single-incubator platform covers one cohort. The analogy only holds at multi-incubator scale. |
| **Negative signals** | Credit bureaus rely heavily on negative data (missed payments, defaults). This platform captures mostly positive signals (reviews, tags, nominations). A credibility bureau without negative data has a blind spot. |
| **Coordination cost** | Multi-incubator adoption is a harder problem than building the platform itself. Competing programs may not want to share data. |
| **Regulatory overhead** | Credit bureaus are regulated (FCRA, CCPA, etc.). Founder credibility is currently unregulated but may attract scrutiny if it becomes market-standard. |

---

## When to Use This Frame

| Context | Use it? | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Investor pitch | Yes | Familiar business model; they immediately see the defensibility and TAM |
| Partnership pitch to other incubators | Yes | Shows the vision beyond a single-program tool; gives them a reason to join |
| Founder-facing product copy | No | Founders don't care about being a "data point" in a bureau — they care about their own track record |
| Press / public positioning | Cautiously | Only after multi-incubator scale is real, otherwise it sounds aspirational |
| Early internal alignment | Sparingly | Don't over-position before the core loop is proven |

The rule: use the credit bureau frame in conversations about *business model and scale*. Use `story.md` in conversations about *product and value to founders*.

---

## Where This Goes

1. **Phase 1 (now):** Single-incubator tool. The platform captures and verifies contributions inside one cohort. The credit bureau analogy is aspirational.
2. **Phase 2 (multi-incubator):** The platform becomes the shared infrastructure across programs. At this point, the analogy starts to earn itself — more incubators mean more data, more data means more value downstream.
3. **Phase 3 (market-wide):** The platform is the default place to verify founder credibility, regardless of which incubator (or none) a founder came from. The analogy is fully realized.
