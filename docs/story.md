# Story

This narrative describes why the platform exists and how it creates value. Use it to align product decisions, pitch the platform to new users, or remind yourself what problem we are solving.

---

## Act 1: The Setup

Founders in a cohort are already writing and connecting. They review vendors, recommend tools, make introductions, and share advice. The incubator watches all of this — it knows which founders excel at fundraising, who has deep vendor expertise, which tools actually deliver. But that knowledge stays inside the incubator. It's scattered, tied to no verified identity, and invisible outside the cohort. When the cohort graduates, most of that knowledge disappears. A founder who is the cohort's go-to expert on legal vendors has no portable way to prove that expertise.

## Act 2: The Tension

Yet the incubator has no efficient way to surface that knowledge at the right moment — connecting a founder to the right recommendation, verifying expertise when someone's fundraising, or retaining institutional memory after a cohort graduates. So when those founders go raise their next round, they have no efficient way to prove their expertise beyond their immediate network. VCs reviewing dozens of founders have no efficient way to verify who actually knows their stuff. Both sides are stuck guessing.

## Act 3: The Opportunity

The gap identified in Act 2 is no longer inevitable. An incubator with the right infrastructure can capture, verify, and surface what its founders already produce. The incubator can now surface the right recommendation for a founder, verify expertise when that founder goes to fundraise, and give VCs a trusted signal they don't have to guess at.

## Act 4: The Resolution

The incubator operates that infrastructure — and it doesn't take much to keep it running. Founders write detailed reviews. Peers mark them helpful. Contribution tags accumulate. Backlinks get verified. Nominations add peer recognition. Every signal feeds into a public profile, a credibility report, and a signed packet that founders can share with anyone. Founders who graduate keep contributing because the platform still carries their track record. New founders join because the directory is already populated and trustworthy. The loop feeds itself — more signals make the platform more valuable, which keeps everyone participating.
