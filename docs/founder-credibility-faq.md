# Founder Credibility FAQ

## What is founder credibility?

Founder credibility is the platform's public-safe summary of useful, verified contribution. It is built from actions that help other founders make better operating decisions, especially vendor decisions.

## What builds credibility?

The primary credibility action is writing verified founder reviews. A strong founder review includes firsthand context, specific details, tradeoffs, outcomes, and any required disclosure.

Other signals can support credibility when they represent completed, useful contribution: contribution tags, helpful votes from peers, profile completeness, verified backlinks, and public-safe profile/report signals.

## Do public reviews build founder credibility?

No. Public reviews can help a vendor's public signal, but they are separate from verified founder reputation. A public review is not tied to the same verified cohort context, quality standard, or founder credibility surface.

## Does review quality count?

Yes. Review quality matters more than raw volume. The platform evaluates whether a review includes useful decision context such as service used, concrete outcome, positives, constructive points, specificity, and disclosure when relevant.

The intended mental model is:

```text
Useful verified reviews -> peer trust + quality signal -> founder credibility
```

## Do helpful votes count?

Yes. Helpful votes show that other founders used a review to evaluate a vendor. They are a peer-validation signal, not just a popularity signal.

## What does Connect contribute?

Connect is currently vendor-centered. It helps founders ask for vendor recommendations, respond to questions about vendor experience, and coordinate selected collaboration surfaces such as guest post exchanges.

Vendor-specific help can strengthen contribution history when it creates useful, verified cohort memory. General founder discussion is not currently a credibility surface.

## Do guest post exchanges build credibility?

Sending, accepting, or declining a guest post exchange should not build credibility by itself. Those actions are too easy to game.

The current app records completed published exchanges as activity. Product-wise, a published exchange with a valid URL can reasonably support credibility as a collaboration or backlink/content signal, especially if verified or admin-approved.

## What does Grow show?

Grow is the private credibility workspace. It shows the founder their contribution trail, credibility factors, impact, profile status, public profile/report links, and recommended next actions.

## What is public and what stays private?

Public surfaces are opt-in and public-safe: founder profile, credibility report, contribution tags, aggregate cohort summaries, vendor public data, and structured data.

Private cohort reviews, vendor requests, invite history, admin activity, and internal discussion context should stay inside the cohort trust boundary.

## How should the product be summarized?

```text
Write + Connect -> useful founder contributions -> credibility signals -> Grow, public profile, credibility report
```

Or, more simply:

> Founders build portable credibility by contributing useful vendor knowledge: writing high-quality verified reviews and helping peers evaluate vendors.
